Chinua Achebe

Poet and Novelist - He is best known for his novel 'Things Fall Apart' (1958)

Here's a time line of Chinua Achebe's life:

  • 1930 - Born 16th of November, in Ogidi (now called Anambra State), Eastern Nigeria.
  • 1936 - He attended St Philips' Central School.
  • 1946 - Achebe was admitted as a Major Scholar at then - University College (now called University of Ibadan).
  • 1950 - Achebe wrote a piece for the University Herald entitled "Polar Undergraduate", his debut as an author.
  • 1951-52 - He served as the Herald's editor.
  • 1953 - He was awarded a second-class degree after his final examinations.
  • 1954 - Achebe started work with the Talks Department at Nigerian Broadcasting Service (NBS).
  • 1956 - he was selected at the Staff School run by the British Broadcasting Corporation (BBC). His first trip outside Nigeria was an opportunity to advance his technical production skills, and to solicit feedback on his novel (which was later split into two books).
  • 1958 - His first book "Things Fall Apart" was published.
  • 1958 - He was promoted at NBS and put in charge of the network's eastern region coverage.
  • 1960 - Achebe was awarded a Rockefeller Fellowship for six months of travel, which he called "the first important perk of my writing career". He set out for a tour of East Africa.
  • 1960 - He published his second novel, "No Longer at Ease", and dedicated it to Christie Okili while they were still dating.
  • 1961 - He married Christiana Chinwe (Christie) Okoli at the Chapel of Resurrection on the campus of the University of Ibadan.
  • 1962 - He attended an executive conference of African writers in English at the Makerere University College in Kampala, Uganda. He met with important literary figures from around the continent and the world, including Ghanaian poet Kofi Awoonor, Nigerian playwright and poet Wole Soyinka, and US poet-author Langston Hughes.
  • 1964 - Achebe's third book, "Arrow of God", was published
  • 1975 - He was presented an honorary doctorate from the University of Stirling and the Lotus Prize for Afro-Asian Writers.
  • 1979 - Achebe was awarded the first-ever Nigerian National Merit Award.
  • 1983 - He became the Deputy National Vice-President of People's Redemption Party (PRP), and He also published a book called The "Trouble with Nigeria".
  • 1990 - Achebe became the Charles P. Stevenson Professor of Languages and Literature at Bard College in Annandale-on-Hudson, New York; he held the position for more than fifteen years.
  • 2007 - Achebe was awarded the Man Booker International Prize.
  • 2010 - Achebe was awarded The Dorothy and Lillian Gish Prize for $300,000, one of the richest prizes for the arts.
  • 2013 - He died at the age of 82 years after a short illness in Boston, United States.

“Achebe, If you had been a lackluster storyteller in your last life, I would have asked you to borrow the tongue of a griot when you come again. But you told wonderful stories. If you had been a traitor to your people, race, nation, I would have asked you to come again with their love flowing in your veins. But you were a patriot. If you had pounded your cultural foo-foo and eaten it alone while your fellow men wallowed in heritage hunger, I would have asked you to come back with selflessness. But you were a cultural philanthropist, So I shall ask you to come again the way you came before. Achebe! Come again, the world awaits your return.”

-- Mallam Denja Abdullahi, Deputy Director, National Council for Arts and Culture, Abuja
